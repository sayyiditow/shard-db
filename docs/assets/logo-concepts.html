shard-db logo concepts

Each concept shown at full size, 32px favicon, and monochrome. Pick a direction; I can refine.

1. Hex shards

Three overlapping hex tiles, lead one bright. Reads as "sharded storage" without saying database. Hex shape is unusual enough to stand out from cylinder-clichés.

favicon (32px) monochrome

Strong: recognisable shape, scales to favicon. Weak: hex tiles are also blockchain trope; we'd want to differentiate.

2. Offset cylinders

Two database-cylinders staggered, suggesting sharded storage. Picks up the classic DB icon language but says "more than one" — the distinguishing feature.

favicon (32px) monochrome

Strong: instantly readable as "database, but sharded." Weak: cylinder-DB icon is everywhere; risk of looking generic.

3. Wordmark with chevron

"shard" in monospace + a vertical-bar separator + "db" in accent colour. No icon — pure typography. Reads as code-adjacent, fits a C project.

favicon: "s/" monochrome

Strong: distinctive, scales beautifully, hard to confuse with competitors. Weak: no icon = harder to make a recognisable favicon. The "s/" works but isn't iconic.

4. Hash-bucket grid

4×4 grid (echoes our 16 splits default), three cells lit in different colours. Visualises hash bucketing literally — shards as discrete cells with data scattered across.

favicon (32px) monochrome

Strong: on-message (literally shows hash-bucketing), distinctive, plays well with multi-colour CI badge bar. Weak: can look like a code-blocks/JIRA roadmap icon. Needs to be clearly grid-not-table.

5. Crystal shard (single)

Faceted gem-like polygon. The "shard" name made literal — a single crystal fragment with multiple faces catching light. Reads as quality / precision.

favicon (32px) monochrome

Strong: beautiful, distinctive, premium feel. Plays the "shard" name straight. Weak: says "crystal" not "database"; risk of reading as a gem/jewelry brand.

6. Split bands (chevron-S)

Stylised "S" formed by horizontal bands offset by chevrons — implies sharding visually with the letterform of "shard". Most abstract option.

favicon (32px) monochrome

Strong: unique mark, scales well, faintly suggests "S" without spelling it. Weak: abstract enough that the "shard" reading is non-obvious; needs the wordmark next to it for context.
